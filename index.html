New York City police misconduct tends to accompany higher rates of misdemeanors

Made possible by the repeal of a decades-old law, a deep-dive into the New York City’s Civilian Complaint Review Board records shows a correlation between the NYPD precincts with the highest crime rate and the greatest amount of police misconduct.

By: Dashiell Allen

11/14/2022

Last month the Legal Aid Society launched the NYC Law Enforcement Look-Up, a ‘one-stop-shop’ database which compiles New York Police Department misconduct records across city agencies, including records maintained by the CCRB.

Years in the making, the database is possible thanks to state legislators' 2020 repeal of 50-A, a law which for 44 years shielded police departments across New York from releasing most disciplinary records.

Comparing the amount of substantiated complaints of police misconduct in every NYC precinct with historic crime data from the NYPD showing the number of misdemeanors issued over the past decade, the precincts with the highest amount of complaints tended to also have higher crime levels.

Out of 74 precincts, the top 10 with the most complaints made up 20 percent of all misdemeanors.

“It doesn’t surprise me one bit,” said Abdul Nasser Rad, the managing director of research and data at the nonprofit Campaign Zero.

“The areas with most historical disinvestment is where there’s most police interactions, and wherever there’s the most police interactions that’s where all the police misconduct is going to be.”

Nasser Rad added that if the precincts were further divided by socio-economic factors "that are highly correlated with historical disinvestment, you’ll probably see that it also relates to similar levels of complaints” relative to misdemeanors.
